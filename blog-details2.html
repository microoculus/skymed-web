WHO Endorses FDA Approval of Twice-Yearly HIV Prevention Injection Lenacapavir (Yeztugo)

The World Health Organization (WHO) has formally welcomed the U.S. Food and Drug Administration’s (FDA) approval of the twice-yearly injectable lenacapavir (under the brand name Yeztugo®) for HIV pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP).

This approval follows the landmark 2024 results from the PURPOSE 1 and PURPOSE 2 trials, which demonstrated extraordinary safety and nearly perfect efficacy. PURPOSE 1 showed 100 % protection in cisgender women in sub-Saharan Africa, while PURPOSE 2 reported that ≥ 99.9 % of participants remained HIV-negative, outperforming traditional daily oral PrEP regimens across diverse global populations.

Administered just twice a year, lenacapavir offers sustained protection and addresses key barriers such as daily pill fatigue, frequent clinic visits, and stigma associated with HIV prevention. WHO issued official PrEP guidelines at the International AIDS Conference in Kigali on July 14, 2025, endorsing lenacapavir as an additional HIV prevention option and emphasizing streamlined HIV testing protocols for long-acting injectable therapies.

Moving forward, this FDA approval paves the way for WHO prequalification, potentially fast-tracking national regulatory authorization and procurement via global health funders like the Global Fund. Gilead Sciences has also committed to voluntary licensing with multiple generic manufacturers to enable broader access in low- and lower-middle-income countries.
