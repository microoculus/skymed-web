FDA Approves Qfitlia for Hemophilia A and B Patients Aged 12 and Older

On March 28, 2025, the FDA approved Qfitlia (fitusiran) for routine use in preventing or reducing bleeding episodes in individuals aged 12 and up with hemophilia A or B, including those with or without factor VIII or IX inhibitors.

Qfitlia offers a key advancement by allowing subcutaneous administration once every two months, making it a less frequent alternative to current treatments. Instead of replacing clotting factors, it works by reducing antithrombin levels, which helps increase thrombin, a critical enzyme in blood clotting.

The drug’s safety and efficacy were confirmed through clinical trials, with participants showing a 71–73% reduction in annual bleeding rates compared to standard on-demand treatments.

Qfitlia includes safety warnings for blood clots, gallbladder disease, and liver toxicity, with monitoring required during treatment. The therapy was granted Orphan Drug and Fast Track designations and is approved for use by Sanofi.
